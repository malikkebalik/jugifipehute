The Meaning Behind The Song: Black Visa by Finesse2Tymes

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Friday, September 27, 2024

As a fan of trap and rap music, I often find myself drawn to songs that have catchy beats and memorable lyrics. One song that has recently caught my attention is “Black Visa” by Finesse2Tymes. This song, featuring Moneybagg Yo, has a unique sound and a captivating message that has resonated with many listeners.

Upon listening to “Black Visa,” the first thing that stands out is the infectious chorus. Finesse2Tymes confidently sings, “Break her, fuck her, leave her, come back and do it again. I’m charging these hoes like a black Visa, unlimited shit coming in.” These lines immediately grab your attention and set the tone for the rest of the song.

The lyrics of “Black Visa” speak to the lifestyle of Finesse2Tymes and his approach to relationships. He describes himself as a player who effortlessly moves from one woman to another. He boasts about his ability to charm women and then leave them, only to return and repeat the cycle. The reference to a “black Visa” signifies his unlimited access to pleasure and material possessions through his interactions with women.

Throughout the verses, Finesse2Tymes and Moneybagg Yo further delve into their experiences with women and the wealth they have acquired. They acknowledge their rise from humble beginnings to a life of luxury, attributing this success to their hustling mentality and street smarts. The lyrics paint a picture of lavish escapades and unapologetic indulgence.

Despite the explicit and boastful nature of the lyrics, there is an underlying sense of self-awareness and reflection in the song. Finesse2Tymes acknowledges that his lifestyle is rooted in his success as an artist, making reference to his rap career. He also addresses the consequences of his actions, such as the drama that arises when encountering past flings in public.

What makes “Black Visa” stand out is its production. The beat, produced by DrumGod, PHIL, and vsonthabeat, is infectious and perfectly complements the braggadocious lyrics. The combination of trap elements and the artists’ confident delivery creates an undeniable energy that grabs the listener’s attention from start to finish.

It is important to note that “Black Visa” is part of Finesse2Tymes’ album titled “90 Days” released on September 2, 2022. The song’s popularity is a testament to Finesse2Tymes’ growing fan base and his ability to create memorable tracks.

As a fan of the genre, “Black Visa” appeals to me because of its undeniable energy, catchy chorus, and the insight it provides into the artist’s life. While the lyrics may be explicit and controversial, they shed light on a lifestyle that some may find intriguing or emblematic of the trap rap culture.

In conclusion, “Black Visa” by Finesse2Tymes featuring Moneybagg Yo is a song that captivates listeners with its infectious beat, memorable lyrics, and braggadocious tone. It offers a glimpse into the artist’s lifestyle and his unabashed approach to relationships. Whether you appreciate the song’s message or not, there is no denying its impact and appeal within the rap music scene.
