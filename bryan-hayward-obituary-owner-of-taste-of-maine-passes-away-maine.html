Bryan Hayward obituary: Owner of Taste of Maine passes away Maine

Posted by Martina Birk on Tuesday, August 6, 2024

The community of Woolwich, Maine, is grieving the unexpected passing of Bryan Hayward, the owner of Residential Construction at Morse and Doak Builders Inc. While the exact cause of his death remains undisclosed at this time, the impact of his loss is profoundly felt. Taste of Maine, a local restaurant, expressed their condolences and prayers for Bryan’s family, emphasizing his role as the “best soccer OFD ever” and his remarkable influence on numerous lives.

Morse & Doak Builders, established in 1976, has been a pillar of the broader Midcoast area, providing a wide array of residential contracting services. The company was co-founded by Elliott Morse, Gregory Doak, Bryan Hayward, and Darius Doak. They have diligently led a team of highly skilled and knowledgeable crew members, undertaking a diverse range of residential projects, including historical renovations, general remodeling, and new home construction.

Bryan Hayward’s journey with Morse & Doak Builders began in 1980 when he joined as a temporary summer worker. Growing up, Bryan assisted his father, Frank, in maintaining the physical facilities of Chop Point Camp and School in Woolwich. This early experience instilled a strong work ethic in Bryan and equipped him with a diverse skill set for various repair techniques. His tenure began part-time at Morse and Doak Woodworkers and gradually evolved into a full-time role during the 1980s. In 1991, Bryan became a member of the ownership group, coinciding with the company’s incorporation as Morse and Doak Builders.

The passing of Bryan Hayward has left a void in the Woolwich community and the residential construction industry. His dedication, skills, and contributions to the growth and success of Morse & Doak Builders Inc. will be fondly remembered and deeply missed.
