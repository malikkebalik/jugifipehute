What is Christopher Masterson’s Net Worth and Salary?

Christopher Masterson, a well-known American actor and DJ, has a net worth of $10 million dollars. He is most recognized for his role in the popular sitcom “Malcolm in the Middle,” where he appeared in 118 of the show’s 151 episodes from 2000 to 2006.

In addition to his work on “Malcolm in the Middle,” Masterson has made guest appearances on various TV shows, including “The Pretender,” “That ’70s Show,” “The Dead Zone,” “White Collar,” and “Haven.” He has also acted in several films, such as “My Best Friend’s Wedding,” “American History X,” “Scary Movie 2,” and “Intellectual Property.”

Interestingly, Masterson is the younger brother of fellow actor Danny Masterson.

Early Life and Career

Christopher Masterson was born on January 22, 1980 on Long Island, New York to parents Peter and Carol. He has an older brother, Danny, who is also an actor and DJ, as well as two half-siblings who are both actors: Jordan and Alanna.

Masterson began his acting career as a child in 1988, with his first role in the television film “Hiroshima Maiden.” He later appeared in Cameron Crowe’s romantic comedy “Singles” in 1992, playing the younger version of Campbell Scott’s character. In 1993, Masterson had a small role as a young Avery Brown in an episode of the sitcom “Murphy Brown.”

Christopher Masterson’s Career in Television

In 1994, Masterson appeared on three television programs, including “Dr. Quinn, Medicine Woman,” “The Road Home,” and “What’z Up?”. Throughout the 90s, he appeared in episodes of several shows, such as “The Client,” “Touched by an Angel,” “The Pretender,” and “Millennium.”

His breakthrough role came in 2000 as Francis in “Malcolm in the Middle,” which he played for seven seasons and appeared in 118 episodes. He also guest-starred in three episodes of “That ’70s Show” and appeared on “The Dead Zone” and “The Wild Thornberrys.” In 2003, he starred in MTV’s adaptation of “Wuthering Heights.”

Other television credits include appearances on “White Collar,” “Men at Work,” and “Haven.”

Christopher Masterson’s Acting Career

In the mid 90s, Masterson appeared in “Cutthroat Island,” “Sunchaser,” “My Best Friend’s Wedding,” “Campfire Tales,” “Girl,” and “American History X.” He began the 2000s with a starring role in “Dragonheart: A New Beginning,” followed by a major role in “Scary Movie 2.” His subsequent credits included “Waterborne,” “Intellectual Property,” “The Art of Travel,” “Made for Each Other,” “Impulse,” “Chapman,” “Bad Roomies,” and “Beneath the Leaves.”

Personal Life

From 1999 to 2007, Masterson was in a relationship with actress Laura Prepon, who starred alongside his brother on “That ’70s Show.” Later, Masterson dated actress Yolanda Pecoraro; the two married in 2019, and had a daughter named Chiara in April of 2021.

Like his brother Danny, Masterson is an adherent of Scientology. Additionally, the brothers have invested in a number of restaurants together.

Christopher Masterson’s Former Los Feliz Home

Christopher Masterson previously owned a traditional-style home in the Los Feliz area of Los Angeles with his then-girlfriend Laura Prepon. The 4,801-square-foot abode boasted four bedrooms and five bathrooms, and included a mahogany-paneled library, formal gardens, and a horseshoe driveway.
