What Happened To Trenton Cannon? His Injury Update 2021

Trenton Cannon of the San Francisco 49ers was rushed off the field in an ambulance after sustaining possible head and neck injuries during the first kickoff return against the Seattle Seahawks.

After colliding with Talanoa Hufanga's thigh, Cannon was smacked by the legs of one of his teammates, twisting his neck into an unnatural position.

Cannon collapsed on the field and remained there until an ambulance arrived. As Cannon was stabilized on a stretcher, his colleagues swarmed the scene.

Kyle Shanahan, the 49ers' head coach, provided an update on running back Trenton Cannon, who was rushed off the field in an ambulance after suffering a head injury on the opening kickoff of the team's defeat to the Seahawks on Sunday.

According to Shanahan, Cannon received a concussion but no further head or neck injuries. Cannon was struck in the head and then hit in a way that twisted his neck unnaturally as he went down while making a tackle on the opening kickoff, so it's excellent news that there's no further injury.

Trenton Cannon Wiki

The New York Jets selected Cannon in the sixth round in the 2018 NFL Draft. Since Kelvin Kinney in 1996, he is the first player from Virginia State to get picked.

He had 113 running yards and one rushing score in his debut season, and 17 catches for 144 receiving yards. On November 1, 2019, Cannon was placed on an injured reserve. The Carolina Panthers released him on August 3, 2020.

On August 4, 2020, the Carolina Panthers claimed Cannon off waivers. The Panthers released him on September 2, 2021. He signed with the Baltimore Ravens on September 8, 2021. On September 14, 2021, he was released.

Cannon was acquired off waivers by the San Francisco 49ers on September 15, 2021.
