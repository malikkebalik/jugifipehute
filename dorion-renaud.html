Dorion Renaud- Biography

Dorion Renaud is the founder and CEO of Buttahskin, as well as a model, actor, and music artist. He is well-known for his modeling profession as well as his effort and dedication to making the skincare brand Buttaskin shine in the international market.

Dorion Renaud has been working as an actor and host for numerous years and has amassed a substantial fortune as a result of his employment. His net worth is expected to be around $1.5 million in 2022. The information, however, is not official.

Dorion Renaud- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Education

Dorion Renaud was born on February 8, 1988, in Texas, United States. He grew up as a single child in his hometown, raised by a black family. He hasn’t revealed much about his family or parents, however, he was born Dorion Standberry and eventually changed his name to Dorion Renaud. Dorion Renaud- Professional Career

He discovered Buttah Skin in 2018 and has been featured in Vogue and on the catwalk. He was continuously on the lookout for suitable skincare products and eventually launched his own line of organic and natural shea goodness. Buttah Beauty is high-end, handcrafted skincare, soap, and body care company. The brand focuses on producing low-cost, organic items. When he originally launched the brand, he invited Beyonce to try out the collection, and her approval gave him the green light to succeed. In 2008, he appeared as an actor in the show College Hill Atlanta, which tracked the lives of students at historically black colleges. Following that, in 2013, he was cast in a tiny role in an episode of the television show Welcome to the Family.

Later the same year, on the NBCU network, he co-hosted the show Extra with Billy Bush. Aside from that, he has been on One’s Hollywood Divas and as Khloe Kardashian’s best buddy on E’s Keeping Up with the Kardashians. He has also worked as a model for companies such as Adidas and Nike. He was recently cast in the show Access Daily, a daytime chat show showcasing celebrity news stories in 2021, and he continues to portray Percy in the television sitcom show In the Cut, which he began performing in 2015. On the radio show The Breakfast Club, he recently discussed his path in creating and establishing his own brand.
