Andy Cohen Net Worth: Find Out How Wealthy The Famous American Television Personality Is

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Thursday, July 4, 2024

Andy Cohen has been in the American show business for a while. He is very popular and has amassed a considerable amount of wealth for himself over the years. Andy Cohen is well known as a television presenter and executive in the United States of America. He is also an advocate for LGBTQ rights.

Andy Cohen net worth

Andy Cohen has a net worth of $50m according to various online sources. He is an American television executive and personality. Renowned for his role as the host and executive producer of the late-night talk show “Watch What Happens Live with Andy Cohen,” which debuted in 2009, he has further made his mark as the creator and executive producer of acclaimed reality TV series such as the “The Real Housewives” franchise, “Project Runway,” and “Queer Eye.”

Beyond his television endeavors, Andy has achieved best-selling author status, penning several books, including “Most Talkative: Stories from the Front Lines of Pop Culture” and “Superficial: More Adventures from the Andy Cohen Diaries.”

Background

Born on June 2, 1968, in St. Louis, Missouri, USA, Andrew Joseph Cohen hails from the Jewish family of Lou Cohen and Evelyn. His early years included a memorable stint at ‘Camp Nebagamon for Boys’ in Lake Nebagamon, Wisconsin.

Andrew pursued his education at ‘Clayton High School,’ graduating in 1986. Subsequently, he enrolled at ‘Boston University,’ earning his BS degree in broadcast journalism. During his time at the university, he contributed as a writer to the independent student newspaper, ‘The Daily Free Press.’

Career

Early in his television career, Andy Cohen kicked off as an intern at CBS News, collaborating with fellow intern Julie Chen. Over the course of a decade, Cohen ascended the ranks at CBS, ultimately achieving the role of senior producer for “The Early Show” and contributing to “48 Hours.” In 2000, he ventured to become the VP of programming for Trio, a cable channel. Bravo later enlisted Cohen’s expertise in reshaping the network into the reality TV powerhouse it is today. Serving as the Executive Vice President of Original Programming and Development for Bravo, Cohen initiated his tenure with the launch of the talk show “Watch What Happens Live” in the summer of 2009, which aired weeknights at midnight.

While Cohen resigned from his executive position in 2013, he retained his role as the host of “Watch What Happens Live” and maintained a popular blog on BravoTv.com. In 2013, he declined to co-host the Miss Universe pageant in Russia due to the country’s anti-gay laws, despite having previously hosted various Miss USA and Miss Universe editions.

Beyond his television endeavors, Cohen delved into the literary world, releasing the best-selling book “Most Talkative: Stories from the Front Lines of Pop Culture” in 2012. Following its success, he published his second memoir, “The Andy Cohen Diaries: A Deep Look at a Shallow Year,” in November 2014, inspired by “The Andy Warhol Diaries.” The book, comprising a year’s worth of personal journal entries, also secured a spot on the New York Times Best Seller list.

Cohen expanded his influence by launching “Radio Andy” on SiriusXM Channel 102 in 2015, curating playlists, hosting Town Halls, and presenting specials like “Radio Andy Theater.” He embarked on a national tour with Anderson Cooper for their stage show AC2, covering various cities. In 2016, Cohen released his third memoir, “Superficial: More Adventures From the Andy Cohen Diaries,” which continued the chronicles of the previous two years and, like its predecessors, achieved New York Times Best Seller status.

Further diversifying his career, Cohen took on the role of hosting the revived “Love Connection” on Fox in May 2017, earning a second season renewal in August of the same year. In 2017, he became the co-host of CNN’s New Year’s Eve coverage with Anderson Cooper. Cohen showcased his versatility by making appearances in TV shows like “Unbreakable Kimmy Schmidt,” “Riverdale,” and as a guest judge on “RuPaul’s Drag Race” in March 2018.

Additionally, Cohen assumed the executive producer role for “The Real Housewives” franchise, overseeing popular iterations such as “The Real Housewives of New Jersey,” “The Real Housewives of Orange County,” “The Real Housewives of Atlanta,” and “The Real Housewives of New York City.” Adding to his repertoire, Cohen lent his voice to the character Isaac Calderon in Marvel’s “Moon Girl and Devil Dinosaur,” which premiered in February 2023.

Awards

Throughout his career, Andy has amassed a collection of accolades. His achievements include winning an Emmy in 2010 as Executive Producer of the reality cooking show “Top Chef.” He earned a Peabody Award in 2004 for his role as Executive Producer of the documentary “The N-Word” and secured another Peabody Award in 2007 for his work on “Project Runway.”

Notably, Andy landed on TV Guide’s prestigious Power List in 2010 and was honored in 2012 as one of GQ’s 25 Best Dressed Men of the Year. His influence extended further, with consecutive appearances from 2012 to 2016 on Out Magazine’s roster of Most Powerful Gay People.

In 2019, Cohen received the esteemed Vito Russo Award at the 30th GLAAD Media Awards, cementing his impact and recognition within the industry.

Andy Cohen Gay

Andy Cohen made history as the inaugural openly gay host of an American late-night talk show. In February 2019, he embraced fatherhood with the assistance of a surrogate, celebrating the arrival of his son, Benjamin Allen Cohen. Subsequently, in April 2022, his daughter, Lucy, was born. Unfortunately, on March 21, 2020, Cohen received a diagnosis of COVID-19.

Social Media

Andy Cohen is active on both Twitter and Instagram where he has more than 6m followers.

Real Estate

Andy’s initial foray into real estate took place in 2003, where he acquired a unit in a West Village building at 2 Horatio Street for an undisclosed sum. Over time, he expanded his real estate portfolio by acquiring three more units in the same building, seamlessly merging them into a unified living space. Presently, this West Village apartment is estimated to be valued between $6 to $8 million.

In March 2019, Andy made another significant real estate move, securing a half-acre oceanfront property with 125 feet of ocean frontage in the Hamptons community of Amagansett for $5.4 million. The initial asking price for the lot was $6 million, having once been listed at $7.3 million.

Fast-forwarding to 2022, Cohen added a New York City penthouse to his real estate holdings, making the purchase for $18.3 million.
